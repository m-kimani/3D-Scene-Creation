# 3D-Scene-Creation
How do I approach designing software?
I start by understanding what I’m trying to build, then break it into smaller parts. I think about how the user will interact with it and keep the design simple and flexible.


What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?
I got better at organizing components, structuring scenes, and thinking ahead about how everything connects instead of just coding randomly.


What design process did you follow for your project work?
I planned the layout and started with one object first, then built a basic version, and kept improving it step by step. I focused on getting things working before making it look better. I then applied what I learned to build the rest of the scene objects.


How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?
Breaking things into smaller parts and building in steps will help me stay organized and avoid getting stuck on big problems.


How do I approach developing programs?
I start simple, get something working, then improve it. I test as I go instead of waiting until the end to keep debugging simple.


What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?
I focused on reusing code, positioning objects more precisely, and adjusting things incrementally to see how the scene was affected instead of guessing.


How did iteration factor into your development?
Iteration was a big part of it. I kept making small changes, testing, and fixing things until my scene looked and worked right with the lighting.


How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones, which led you to the project’s completion?
At first I was guessing more, but over time I became more structured. I planned better, made cleaner changes, and avoided breaking things.
